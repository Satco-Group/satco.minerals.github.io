  <h1>Fluorspar (Fluorite - CaF₂)</h1>
  <p>Fluorspar is the commercial name for the mineral fluorite (CaF₂), a critical industrial mineral valued for its high fluorine content. It serves as a vital flux in steel production, a source of hydrofluoric acid for chemical industries, and a key component in the manufacturing of aluminum and ceramics.</p>

  <h2>Key Properties</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>High Purity (CaF₂):</b> Optimized concentration to ensure effective chemical reaction as a flux.</li>
    <li><b>Low Impurities:</b> Minimal silica and sulfur levels to prevent detrimental effects in downstream metallurgical processes.</li>
    <li><b>Consistency:</b> Uniform granule size and mineralogical composition.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Advantages & Disadvantages</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Advantages:</b> Excellent fluxing ability, lowering the melting point of slag and increasing its fluidity.</li>
    <li><b>Disadvantages:</b> Sensitive to humidity if stored improperly; naturally brittle nature.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Applications</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Metallurgy:</b> Acts as a flux in steelmaking and metal smelting to remove impurities.</li>
    <li><b>Chemical Industry:</b> Primary raw material for the production of Hydrogen Fluoride (HF) and fluorine-based chemicals.</li>
    <li><b>Aluminum Industry:</b> Used in the production of aluminum fluoride (AlF₃).</li>
    <li><b>Ceramics/Glass:</b> Employed as an opacifier and flux in glass and enamel production.</li>
  </ul>
