  <h1>Gypsum Powder (Plaster of Paris)</h1>
  <p>Gypsum powder, produced through the calcination of high-quality gypsum rock, is a fundamental material in the construction and manufacturing industries. Known for its quick setting time and exceptional workability, it is essential for finishing works and specialized casting applications.</p>

  <h2>Key Properties</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Controlled Setting Time:</b> Optimized for initial and final setting requirements.</li>
    <li><b>Fine Particle Size:</b> Ensures a smooth, high-quality surface finish.</li>
    <li><b>High Whiteness:</b> Derived from pure raw material for aesthetic consistency.</li>
    <li><b>Thermal/Acoustic Insulation:</b> Acts as a natural insulator for interior spaces.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Advantages & Disadvantages</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Advantages:</b> Superior adhesion to substrates, high plasticity for decorative details, and rapid drying.</li>
    <li><b>Disadvantages:</b> Highly sensitive to humidity; requires moisture-proof storage; lower structural load-bearing capacity compared to cement.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Applications</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Construction:</b> Interior wall plastering, ceiling finishes, and decorative moldings.</li>
    <li><b>Casting:</b> Production of precise molds for ceramics and pottery.</li>
    <li><b>Medical:</b> Used in the production of medical-grade orthopedic casts.</li>
    <li><b>Industrial:</b> Filler in various specialized chemical and artistic applications.</li>
  </ul>
